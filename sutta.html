Sutta: Discourses of the Buddha

Explore the profound teachings of the Buddha through the Suttas, which contain the core principles of Buddhism and guide practitioners on the path to enlightenment.

Introduction to Sutta

What are Suttas?

Suttas are the discourses of the Buddha, containing his teachings on various aspects of life, ethics, meditation, and the path to enlightenment.

Learn More

Historical Context

The Suttas were originally transmitted orally and later compiled into collections, forming a significant part of the Buddhist canon.

Explore History

Importance in Buddhist Practice

Suttas provide guidance for daily life, meditation practice, and understanding the nature of reality according to Buddhist philosophy.

Discover Importance

Key Teachings in Sutta

The Four Noble Truths

The foundational teaching of Buddhism, explaining the nature of suffering and the path to its cessation.

Explore Noble Truths

The Noble Eightfold Path

A practical guide to ethical and mental development, leading to liberation from suffering.

Understand the Path

Dependent Origination

Major Sutta Collections

Digha Nikaya

The collection of long discourses, containing 34 suttas on various topics.

Explore Digha Nikaya

Majjhima Nikaya

The collection of middle-length discourses, with 152 suttas of moderate length.

Study Majjhima Nikaya

Samyutta Nikaya

The collection of connected discourses, organized by topic with over 2,900 suttas.

Discover Samyutta Nikaya

Anguttara Nikaya

The collection of numerical discourses, with suttas grouped by number of items.

Explore Anguttara Nikaya

Khuddaka Nikaya

The collection of minor texts, including popular works like the Dhammapada.

Learn Khuddaka Nikaya
