Abhidhamma: Higher Teachings of Buddhism

Explore the profound philosophical and psychological aspects of Buddhism through the lens of Abhidhamma, the higher doctrine that offers a detailed analysis of mind and matter.

Introduction to Abhidhamma

What is Abhidhamma?

Abhidhamma, meaning 'higher doctrine', is the third basket of the Tipitaka, the canonical scriptures of Theravada Buddhism. It offers a detailed psychological and philosophical analysis of the nature of reality.

Learn More

Historical Context

The Abhidhamma was developed and systematized by early Buddhist scholars, elaborating on the Buddha's teachings to create a comprehensive framework for understanding the mind and reality.

Explore History

Importance in Buddhist Study

Abhidhamma provides a theoretical foundation for Buddhist practice, offering deep insights into the nature of consciousness, mental factors, and the process of liberation.

Discover Importance

Key Concepts in Abhidhamma

Consciousness (Citta)

Abhidhamma analyzes consciousness into various types and states, providing a detailed map of the mind's workings.

Explore Consciousness

Mental Factors (Cetasika)

These are the various mental qualities that arise with consciousness, shaping our experiences and reactions.

Understand Mental Factors

Matter (Rupa)

Abhidhamma explores the nature of physical phenomena and how they interact with mental phenomena.

Learn About Matter

Dependent Origination

This fundamental Buddhist concept is examined in great depth, revealing the interconnected nature of all phenomena.

Discover Interconnectedness

The Seven Books of Abhidhamma

1. Dhammasangani

Enumeration of Phenomena: Classifies consciousness and mental factors.

Explore Dhammasangani

2. Vibhanga

The Book of Analysis: Analyzes various doctrinal topics.

Study Vibhanga

3. Dhatukatha

Discourse on Elements: Discusses elements of existence.

Understand Dhatukatha

4. Puggalapannatti

Designation of Human Types: Describes various personality types.

Explore Human Types

5. Kathavatthu

Points of Controversy: Discusses doctrinal differences among early Buddhist schools.

Examine Controversies

6. Yamaka

The Book of Pairs: Presents doctrinal topics in pairs of questions.

Study Pairs

7. Patthana

Conditional Relations: Explores the complex web of causal relations in reality.

Discover Causality
